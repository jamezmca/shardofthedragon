## 6. Factorising Quadratics

**L1 ·** Find two numbers that multiply to the constant and add to the \(x\) coefficient. &ensp; \(x^2+7x+12\): 3×4=12, 3+4=7 → \((x+3)(x+4)\)

**L2 ·** One bracket adds, one subtracts; the larger number keeps the sign that matches the middle term. &ensp; \(x^2+3x-10\): 5×2=10, 5−2=3 → \((x+5)(x-2)\)

**L3 ·** Both brackets subtract; the pair multiplies to a positive last term. &ensp; \(x^2-7x+12\): 3×4=12, 3+4=7 → \((x-3)(x-4)\)

## 12. Inequalities

**L1 ·** Solve exactly like an equation — the inequality sign replaces =. &ensp; \(x+3>7 \Rightarrow x>4\)

**L2 ·** Two-step solve; sign stays the same when dividing by a positive. &ensp; \(3x+2>11 \Rightarrow 3x>9 \Rightarrow x>3\)

**L3 ·** Collect \(x\) terms to one side; sign only flips if you divide by a negative. &ensp; \(5x+2>2x+8 \Rightarrow 3x>6 \Rightarrow x>2\)

## 13. Completing the Square

**L1 ·** Halve the \(x\) coefficient to get \(b\), then subtract \(b^2\). &ensp; \(x^2+6x=(x+3)^2-9\)

**L2 ·** Same method, then tack the constant back on at the end. &ensp; \(x^2+6x+2=(x+3)^2-9+2=(x+3)^2-7\)

**L3 ·** Negative middle — the bracket subtracts instead of adds. &ensp; \(x^2-8x+3=(x-4)^2-16+3=(x-4)^2-13\)

## 14. Simultaneous Equations

**L1 ·** Both equations equal \(y\) — set the right-hand sides equal and solve. &ensp; \(y=3x+1,\ y=x+5 \Rightarrow x=2,\ y=7\)

**L2 ·** Eliminate one variable by adding or subtracting the two equations. &ensp; \(2x+y=7\) and \(x+y=4\): subtract → \(x=3\), back-sub → \(y=1\)
